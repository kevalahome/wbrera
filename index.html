⚖️ What is RERA?

The Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016 protects homebuyers by requiring developers to register projects and maintain transparency on delivery timelines, fund usage, and approvals.

A valid RERA registration means the developer is legally accountable. Always verify before paying any advance amount.

📋 Buyer Safety Checklist

10 things to verify before paying any advance on a property

✅
RERA Registration is Active
Verify on the official WB RERA portal. Active means the developer is legally accountable for delivery.
📅
Completion Date is in the Future
Check the promised possession date. If already past, request a written revised timeline before paying.
💳
70% Funds in Escrow
Under RERA, 70% of buyer funds must be deposited in a designated account used only for this project.
📜
Land Title is Clear
Ask for an encumbrance certificate. Confirm no legal disputes. Hire a property lawyer to verify title.
🏗️
Building Plan Approved
Ensure KMDA, BIADA, or relevant authority has approved the plan. Ask for the approval number.
📖
Read the Sale Agreement
Don't sign without reading every clause. Check for delay penalty clauses and refund conditions.
🏦
Project is Bank-Approved
If SBI, HDFC, or ICICI are offering home loans here, it signals their due diligence has been done.
⚠️
Check RERA Complaints
Search the RERA portal for complaints against this developer or project. Multiple complaints = red flag.
🔌
Utility Approvals in Place
Confirm water, electricity, and sewerage approvals are obtained or in progress from authorities.
🤝
Developer Track Record
Research previous projects. Visit a completed project if possible and speak to residents there.
